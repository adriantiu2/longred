Long Red - Mountain (1972)

This website is an attempt to explain a single song’s legacy through the context of sampling. “Long Red” by Mountain (1972) is a song Leslie West performed with his band at Woodstock in 1969. The live recording of this song was released on the album “Live: The Road Goes Ever On”. The Mountain Live version has one of the most sampled drum breaks in the history of hip hop music. The break (as well as West's banter with the audience) from the live version has been sampled in over 780 songs as of 2021.

The three main snippets of this song that have historically been sampled are a “Lowdah!” interjection that appears 0:07, a “Yeahhh!” interjection that appears at 0:22 and the drums which appear throughout, but are best isolated at 0:01.

The pixels that appear as you click through the three major snippets of the song eventually culminate to create the cover art for “Long Red”. Since this website only includes a fraction of the samples (100/788), the pixelation of the cover shows that this is an incomplete archive. The 100 included songs are chosen based on an order of relevance provided by whosampled.com.
